## Overview

Vala reports turn available records into structured working documents. Report types may vary by organization and workflow.

## When to use

Use Reports when a structured draft can support your review or claim-preparation work.

## Prerequisites

- The correct veteran and matter
- The relevant source records
- Any organization-specific direction for the report

## Step-by-step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose the appropriate report">Select the report that matches the work you are performing.</Step>
  <Step title="Generate the working draft">Use the records available in the veteran workspace.</Step>
  <Step title="Review the sources">Confirm material dates, events, and findings against the underlying record.</Step>
  <Step title="Apply professional judgment">Correct, refine, or remove anything unsupported before use.</Step>
</Steps>

## Best practices

- Treat every generated report as a draft.
- Review citations and surrounding source context.
- Keep human approval in the final workflow.

## Common mistakes

<Warning>Do not share, file, or rely on a generated report without independent review.</Warning>

- Generating a report before the relevant records are available
- Leaving unsupported statements in the draft
